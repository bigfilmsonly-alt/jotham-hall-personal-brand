## Long Description / Full Biography
Jotham Hall was born and raised in Oakland, California on September 13, 1984. He attended an ecology-based academy called Common Ground during his high school years, where he traveled internationally, learned about new cultures, and developed tangible life skills at a young age.

After high school, Jotham worked in his family's construction business as a builder's apprentice. His passion for the film industry led him to attend college in Santa Barbara to study film production, media, and marketing.

In 2008, Jotham moved to Hollywood to pursue a career in the entertainment industry. He landed a job with 51 Minds Entertainment, a production company, and quickly adapted and rose through the ranks as a talent producer. Over the next 7 years, he traveled the world, managed top 40 music artists, and contributed to the creative process of over 50 hit reality TV shows such as MTV's "G's to Gents," "Flavor of Love," and "I Love Money."

In 2015, Jotham moved to Hawaii to raise his family and cultivate a newfound love for business. He launched Big Films Only, a black-owned, family-owned, independent production company. As a solo-entrepreneur, he quickly developed marketing and sales skills and was able to utilize his experience in the film industry for his own business.

Today, Jotham is based in Miami Beach, Florida and is involved with major TV productions such as USA Network's "Temptation Island," NBC's "Snake in the Grass," and BET's "After Happily Ever After" hosted by platinum recording artist Bow Wow. He has also founded Success Upgrade (an AI-powered ecosystem for entrepreneurs) and Say It Build It (an AI development platform).

Jotham is passionate about sustainability, creating social change, developing eco-friendly housing, and giving people an opportunity to tell their story. His unique background in construction, film production, and marketing has allowed him to become a successful entrepreneur dedicated to making a positive impact on society.
